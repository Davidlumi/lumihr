# lumi Data Contribution Terms

**Version 1.0**

These terms are accepted once, by an Admin, on behalf of the contributing
organisation. They govern how your organisation's data is used in the lumi
benchmarking co-operative. The full Data Sharing Agreement (DPA) expands on
these terms and is available to download for your legal or data-protection team.

## What you contribute
- Organisation-level reward data: policies, practices and aggregate figures.
- No employee-level or personal data is requested, required, or knowingly stored.
  Don't submit personal data in free-text fields.

## How your data is used
- Your answers are combined into anonymised, aggregated benchmarks.
- **Suppression rule:** no aggregate is ever shown unless at least 5
  organisations contribute to it. Your individual answers are never shown to
  any other member.
- Peer groups are described by attributes (industry, size band); contributing
  organisations are never named to other members.

## Where your data lives
- Data is hosted in the EU/UK and never transferred outside without equivalent
  safeguards.
- Your data is never sold, never shared with third parties for their own use,
  and never used to train external AI models.

## Your rights as a member
- You can export your organisation's own submitted data at any time ("My data").
- You can ask lumi to delete your organisation's data; it is removed from
  future benchmark snapshots after deletion.
- Aggregates already published to other members (which never identify you)
  are not recalled.

## Your commitments
- Submitted data is accurate to the best of your organisation's knowledge.
- You have your organisation's authority to contribute it.

## Term
- These terms apply while your organisation is a member and survive for data
  retention/deletion obligations afterwards.
- Governing law: England and Wales.

*The acceptance of these terms is recorded (organisation, accepting Admin,
date, version) and constitutes the organisational agreement.*
